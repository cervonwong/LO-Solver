# Core Grammatical Generalizations

1.  **Word Order:** Subject – Object – Locative – Verb. Adjective precedes the noun.
2.  **Noun Structure:** Root – Number – Case.
    *   **Number:**
        *   sg = Ø
        *   pl = `-’` (exception: people = `entʃu’`)
    *   **Case:**
        *   in = `-n`
        *   to = `-d`
        *   from = `-xuth` (`-’` + `xuth` > `guth`)
3.  **Verb Structure:** Root — Auditive — Subject — Tense
    *   **Auditive:** `munu` (the subject hearing is marked on the verb; pronoun subject is not written)
    *   **Subject Suffixes:**
        | | 1 | 2 | 3 |
        | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
        | **sg** | `-th’` | `-d` | `Ø` |
        | **pl** | `-ba’` | `-ra’` | `-’` |
    *   **Tense:**
        *   present = `Ø`
        *   past = `-bi` (`-’` + `bi` > `pi`)

# Part 1

**What is the literal translation of "Enets people" in Forest Enets?**
*   **Answer:** “Real people”

# Part 2 (Translate into English)

**13. Forest Enets: `Bodu soitha sɛsur katha.`**
*   **English:** The tundra kills the good fox.

**14. Forest Enets: `Mudyna’ bodud kanyimunu’.`**
*   **English:** They hear us going to the tundra.

**15. Forest Enets: `Kothu’ aga entʃi’ aga oduguth mopi.`**
*   **English:** The snowstorms took the big person from the big boats.

**16. Forest Enets: `Uuda’ mudy dyurirapi odu onai dyotud tomunu.`**
*   **English:** You (pl) told me that he hears the boat coming to the real goose.

**17. Forest Enets: `Uu budu’ dyurimunuth’ aga entʃi’ onai bathan dyuriŋa.`**
*   **English:** I hear you (sg) telling them that the big person speaks Enets.

# Part 3 (Translate into Forest Enets)

**18. English: You (sg) speak the language.**
*   **Answer:** `Uu bathan dyuriŋad.`

**19. English: The geese hear me running to the good reindeer (sg).**
*   **Answer:** `Dyotu’ mudy soitha ted nɛbimunu’.`

**20. English: We tell them that you (pl) took the goose from the big fox.**
*   **Answer:** `Mudyna’ budu’ dyuriba’ uuda’ dyotu aga sɛsurxuth morapi.`

**21. English: They took the boat from the real reindeer (pl).**
*   **Answer:** `Budu’ odu onai teguth mopi.`

**22. English: He heard the snowstorm coming from the tundra.**
*   **Answer:** `Kothu boduxuth tomunubi.`
